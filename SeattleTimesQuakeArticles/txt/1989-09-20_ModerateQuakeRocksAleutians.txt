Moderate quake    rocks Aleutians    PALMER, Alaska       A moderately strong earthquake measuring 5.8 on the Richter scale rumbled this morning off Alaska's Aleutian Islands, but there were no reports of damage or of a seismic sea wave        The Tsunami Warning Center said the quake occurred at 5:19 a.m. Alaska Daylight Time and pinpointed it at 130 miles south of Amchitka and 260 miles southwest of Adak Island. It was strongly felt in Amchitka, the center said.        An earthquake of magnitude 5 on the Richter scale, a measure of ground motion as recorded on seismographs, can cause considerable damage in an area; a quake measuring 6 can be severe.
