PUYALLUP - A mild earthquake rumbled through the Puyallup area early today, the University of Washington geophysics center reported.    The magnitude 3.2 quake struck at 7:10 a.m. It was centered 7 1/2 miles east-southeast of Tacoma, at a depth of about 13 miles, the center said.    No damage was reported.    An earthquake of magnitude 3.5 can cause slight damage.
