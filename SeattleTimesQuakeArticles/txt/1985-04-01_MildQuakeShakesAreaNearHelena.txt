GOLDEN, Colo. (AP) _ An earthquake shook the mountains northwest of Helena, Mont., near the Continental Divide at 2:13 a.m. today, officials at the National Earthquake Center said.        The quake, which registered 4.6 on the Richter scale, had its epicenter in a sparsely populated area 70 miles from Helena, said seismologists. There were no reports of damage.        The quake was felt within a radius of about 100 miles, in Great Falls, Kalispell, Missoula and Helena.
