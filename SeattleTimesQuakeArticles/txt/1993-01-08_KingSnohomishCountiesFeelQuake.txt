SEATTLE       A mild earthquake that measured 3.5 on the Richter scale, a measure of ground motion recorded on seismographs, shook eastern King and Snohomish counties yesterday afternoon, the University of Washington seismology laboratory said     The UW lab said the quake occurred at 2:06 p.m. and was centered 10 1/2 miles southwest of Skykomish, nine miles beneath the Earth's surface.     No damage or injuries were reported.     The quake was felt in Sultan, Gold Bar and the North Bend areas, the lab said.
