SANTA BARBARA, Calif. _ A mild earthquake followed by an aftershock rumbled through several Southern California coastal communities early yesterday, rattling some people out of bed but causing no damage or injuries        The quake, 3.9 on the Richter scale, hit at 12:42 a.m. and was followed by a 3.0 aftershock at 12:57 a.m., officials at the U.S. Geological Survey in Golden, Colo., said.        ``We pinpointed it in the waters about 20 miles south of Santa Barbara,'' said Waverly Person, a geophysicist at the earthquake center.
