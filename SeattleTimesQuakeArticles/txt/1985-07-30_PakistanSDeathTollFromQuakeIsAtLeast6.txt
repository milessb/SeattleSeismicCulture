ISLAMABAD, Pakistan (AP) _ Officials said today the death toll from an earthquake that rocked Afghanistan and northern Pakistan, now at least six, could rise as communication is reopened with remote areas cut off by landslides        More than 30 other people were injured by the quake yesterday, which collapsed dozens of home and shops. Seismological institutes said it measured about 7 on the Richter scale.        Government officials said five people were reported killed in mountainous northern Pakistan when their homes collapsed. A sixth person died in Rawalpindi, which adjoins Pakistan's capital of Islamabad, according to the government-owned Pakistan Times newspaper.        The quake's epicenter was in the Hindu Kush mountains in northeastern Afghanistan, said Don Finley, spokesman for the U.S. Geological Survey in Golden, Colo.
