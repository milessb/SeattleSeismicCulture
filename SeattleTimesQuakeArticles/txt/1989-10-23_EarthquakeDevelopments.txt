Earthquake developments    -----------------------------------    --  DAMAGE      The damage estimate has risen to $7.1 billion, which would make the earthquake the most expensive natural disaster in U.S. history.      The American Red Cross says at least 729 homes and residential buildings were destroyed. The California Office of Emergency Services estimates that more than 105,000 houses, 320 apartment buildings and 1,345 businesses were damaged.      The Red Cross said more than 10,000 people took refuge in its 19 shelters..    --  CASUALTIES      Sixty-one deaths have been confirmed, 38 in the collapse of Interstate 880. The state OES reports that 3,011 people were injured.    --  THE QUAKE AND    AFTERSHOCKS      The earthquake hit at 5:04 p.m. Tuesday, registered 6.9 on the Richter scale and was centered on the San Andreas Fault, 14 miles northeast of Santa Cruz and 57 miles south of San Francisco in the Santa Cruz Mountains.      More than 2,000 aftershocks have been recorded.    --  FEDERAL ASSISTANCE      President Bush signed a disaster-relief declaration for seven Bay Area counties and authorized an initial $273 million in aid.    --  HIGHWAY CLOSURES      The San Francisco-Oakland Bay Bridge, for at least four weeks after one section collapsed. I-880, from the Bay Bridge south to downtown Oakland. Embarcadero Freeway in San Francisco, until June because of severe cracking. Highway 101, northbound at Highway 92 overpass. Highway 17 in Santa Cruz Mountains, from Scotts Valley to Highway 9, for an estimated three weeks due to slides. Highway 25, from Highway 101 to 15 miles south of Hollister in San Benito County, due to slides.    --  QUAKE HOTLINE      The San Francisco Earthquake and Relief Assistance Hotline is 1-900-500-HELP. It offers news updates and status of transportation. Each call costs $5, with net proceeds donated to the Red Cross.      The Red Cross says a number will be set up next week for inquiries about quake victims, but information is not being given out now.    --  WEATHER      Rain was heavy today  and 40-mph winds were reported.    -----------------------------------------------------------    Associated Pres
