PALMER, Alaska - A strong earthquake rattled south-central Alaska yesterday, but there were no immediate reports of damage.    The quake had a magnitude of 6.2 and occurred in Cook Inlet about 55 miles southwest of Homer, said Wayne Jorgenson of the Tsunami Warning Center in Palmer. It struck at 11:14 a.m.    The quake was felt across a wide area of south-central Alaska, including Homer, Kenai, Anchorage and Kodiak, Jorgenson said
