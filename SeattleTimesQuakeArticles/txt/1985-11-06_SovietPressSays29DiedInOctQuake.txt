MOSCOW _ A Soviet newspaper said today that 29 people died in last month's earthquake in the Central Asia Republic of Tadzhikstan, the first time official casualty figures for the disaster have been released        The daily Socialist Industry said figures were released to end "distortions and rumors." It said more than 80 people were injured.        The quake, which struck Kayrakkum and surrounding areas on the night of Oct. 13, measured 6.1 points on the open-ended Richter scale, according to Moscow's Seismological Institute.        It was the Soviet Union's largest known quake since 1976, when neighboring Uzbekistan was hit.
