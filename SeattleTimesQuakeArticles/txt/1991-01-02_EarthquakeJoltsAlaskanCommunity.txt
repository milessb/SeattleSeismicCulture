Earthquake jolts    Alaskan community    PALMER, Alaska        Alaska started off 1991 with a seismic jolt.        Bob Hammond, a geophysicist with the Palmer-based Tsunami Warning Center, reported that a moderate quake early New Year's Day measured 5.2 on the Richter scale and was centered about 90 miles southeast of Sand Point.        There were no immediate reports of injury or damage.        Sand Point is an island fishing community of about 900 people off the Alaska Peninsula. It is 250 miles northeast of Dutch Harbor.
