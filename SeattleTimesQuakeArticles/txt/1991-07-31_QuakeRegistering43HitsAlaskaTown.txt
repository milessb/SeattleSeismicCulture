PALMER, Alaska      An earthquake registering 4.3 on the Richter scale shook the Alaskan town of Seward, but there were no reports of injury or damage, according to the Sunami Warning Center.      The tremor yesterday morning was centered about 40 miles east of Seward, where residents reported feeling the shaking.      Alaska's strongest earthquake, in 1964, registered 8.5 on the open-ended Richter scale. Times staff, news service
