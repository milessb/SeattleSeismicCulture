ANCHORAGE, Alaska      A moderate earthquake yesterday shook people awake on the remote Alaska Peninsula, but there were no reported injuries or damage.    The quake, given a preliminary reading of 5.2 on the Richter scale, hit at 9:22 a.m., said George Serta at the Alaska Tsunami Warning Center in Palmer. It was centered 80 miles southwest of Sand Point, about 640 miles southwest of Anchorage.    An aftershock three minutes later measured 4.8 on the Richter scale.    Magnitude-5 quakes are capable of causing considerable damage in densely populated areas.
