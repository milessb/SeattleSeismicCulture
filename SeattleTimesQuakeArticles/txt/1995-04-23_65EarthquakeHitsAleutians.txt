PALMER, Alaska - A large earthquake with a preliminary magnitude of 6.5 rocked an area off the sparsely populated Aleutian Islands yesterday, but no damage or injuries were reported.    The quake, located 155 miles southwest of Adak near the western tip of the island chain, struck at 6:56 p.m. ADT and was strongly felt by local residents, said Wayne Jorgensen of the Tsunami Warning Center. There was no danger of a tsunami, he said
