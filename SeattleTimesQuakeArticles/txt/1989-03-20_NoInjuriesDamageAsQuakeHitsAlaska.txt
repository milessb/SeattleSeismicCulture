NO INJURIES, DAMAGE AS QUAKE HITS ALASKA    --  ANCHORAGE        A powerful earthquake rattled southern Alaska yesterday afternoon, but there were no reports of damage or injuries, authorities said.        The U.S. Geological Survey in Golden, Colo., said the tremor, centered about 170 miles southwest of Anchorage, measured 5.3 on the Richter scale..        Although it was widely felt from north of Anchorage to Kodiak, a large island about 150 miles south of the epicenter, there was no damage, according to a spokesman for the state Office of Emergency Services.
