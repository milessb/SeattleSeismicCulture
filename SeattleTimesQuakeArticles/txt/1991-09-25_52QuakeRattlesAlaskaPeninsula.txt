COLD BAY, Alaska      A moderate earthquake with a preliminary magnitude of 5.2 on the Richter scale shook the southern Alaska peninsula at 12:05 p.m. yesterday.      The Tsunami Warning Center in Palmer reported that the quake was centered in the North Pacific about 100 miles southwest of Cold Bay. The earthquake was felt slightly at Cold Bay and False Pass.      There was no tsunami generated, and no damages or injuries reported, said center spokesman Wayne Jorgensen.
