Grocery clerk Kevin Weckerle surveys the damage in a Los Gatos, Calif., market after an earthquake measuring 5.1 on the Richter scale hit the San Francisco Bay area early this morning. The quake caused similar damage - mostly broken glass - throughout the region, but only one casualty. A 19-year-old Los Gatos youth who apparently was startled awake died after he either fell or leaped from a fifth-story window The quake was centered on the San Andreas fault about 13 miles south of San Jose. Seismologists issued a 10-day alert for aftershocks.Caption:  PHOTOAP
