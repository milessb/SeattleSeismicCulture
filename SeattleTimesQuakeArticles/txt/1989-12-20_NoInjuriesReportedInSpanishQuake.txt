MADRID, Spain, - An earthquake shook the southern Spanish region of Andalucia early today, but there were no immediate reports of injury or serious damage, police said.        The tremor measured 5.2 on the Richter scale and was centered in the Gulf of Cadiz. It lasted between three and five seconds.        Police said the impact was strongest in the province of Huelva, where residents in several towns fled into the streets. There were also reports of buildings swaying in Seville, Cordoba and Cadiz.
