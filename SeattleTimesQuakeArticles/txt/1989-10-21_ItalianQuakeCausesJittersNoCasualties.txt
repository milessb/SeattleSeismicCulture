ITALIAN QUAKE CAUSES JITTERS, NO CASUALTIES    ROME         An early-morning quake shook the hill country south of Rome, causing panic and minor structural damage to buildings, but authorities said no injuries were reported         Local police and fire departments said hundreds of residents of the Alban Hills called in to say they were frightened by the temblor, which lasted about 10 seconds.         The temblor, which shook an area about 15 miles south of Rome, registered 6 to 7 on the 12-point Mercalli scale. An earthquake of that magnitude can cause serious damage. The Mercalli and Richter scales are not convertible.
