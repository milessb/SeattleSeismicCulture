BELGRADE, Yugoslavia - The Serb-led Yugoslav national soccer team, banned from international competition, has offered to play a benefit match in Los Angeles for victims of the Southern California earthquake    Soccer officials said a proposal was sent to U.S. soccer authorities. No date was specified.    "The friendly game would have entirely a humanitarian dimension," said Yugoslav Manager Miljan Miljanic. "Americans have helped us in the similar situations in the past, and we want to repay them this way."     Yugoslavia, consisting of Serbia and tiny Montenegro, came under United Nations sanctions in May 1992 for aggressive actions in neighboring Croatia and Bosnia-Herzegovina. Its national teams have been banned from international play.
