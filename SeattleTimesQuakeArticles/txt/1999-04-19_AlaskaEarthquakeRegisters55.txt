PALMER, Alaska - A moderate earthquake shook Alaska's Kenai Peninsula yesterday morning, the West Coast-Alaska Tsunami Warning Center said.    Seismologist Guy Urban said the 7:06 a.m. quake had a preliminary measurement of 5.5, and was widely felt in Homer, Kenai and Palmer.    Urban said the quake was centered about 45 miles north of Homer. There were no immediate reports of damage, he said.
