SMALL QUAKE SHAKES SUBURBS SOUTH OF SEATTLE    --  SEATTLE         A small earthquake rattled suburbs south of Seattle but there were no reports of damage or injuries, authorities said.        The quake, which was felt in Federal Way, Renton, Enumclaw and Skyway, occurred at 10:48 last night and registered 3.0 on the Richter scale, according to the National Earthquake Information Center at Golden, Colo.        State Patrol offices in Bellevue and Tacoma said they received no phone calls about the quake from residents.        The last quake in the Puget Sound area occurred a week ago and was centered near Mount Vernon, about 60 miles north of Seattle. It measured 4.0 on the Richter scale and also caused no damage or injuries.
