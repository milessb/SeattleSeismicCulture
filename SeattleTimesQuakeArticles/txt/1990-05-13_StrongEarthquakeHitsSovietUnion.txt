WASHINGTON - A strong earthquake shook a large island in the western portion of the Soviet Union yesterday, the U.S. Geological Survey said.        The temblor measured 6.7 on the Richter scale, occurred at 9:50 a.m. PDT, said Don Kelly, a spokesman for the USGS.        The quake's epicenter was Sakhalin Island, located 900 miles north of Tokyo, said Kelly.
