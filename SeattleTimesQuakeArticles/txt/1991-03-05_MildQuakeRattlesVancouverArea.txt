A mild earthquake early today rattled residents near this southwestern Washington city but no damage was reported.        The 12:41 a.m. quake had a preliminary magnitude of 3 on the Richter scale, said Steve Malone of the University of Washington Geophysics Center in Seattle.         The quake was centered near the Columbia River about nine miles north of Vancouver and 17 miles north of Portland, Ore.        Several residents in the Vancouver area reported feeling the temblor, Malone said.
