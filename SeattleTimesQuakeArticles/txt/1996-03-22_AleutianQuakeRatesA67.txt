PALMER, Alaska - There were no immediate reports of injury or damage after a strong earthquake rattled the Aleutian Islands last night, the Alaska Tsunami Warning Center said.    The 6.7-magnitude quake was centered 210 miles southwest of the town of Adak in the sparsely populated chain of islands off the southwestern tip of Alaska. It struck at 6:24 p.m. Adak is 1,200 miles southwest of Anchorage.
