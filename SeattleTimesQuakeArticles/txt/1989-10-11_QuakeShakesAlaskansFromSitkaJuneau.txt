Quake shakes    Alaskans from    Sitka to Juneau    PALMER, Alaska         A moderate earthquake shook buildings in Alaska's capital and woke up residents from Juneau to Sitka early today. No injuries or damage were immediately reported        The quake measured 5.1 on the Richter scale in a preliminary measurement, said Alec Medbery, spokesman for the Alaska Tsunami Warning Center in Palmer.        It struck about 12:50 a.m. and was centered 50 miles west of Sitka, about 110 miles southwest of Juneau, Medbery said.        ``It was felt widely, all the way from Sitka to Juneau and all points in between,'' Medbery said. ``There were no reports of damage but it woke quite a few people up.'
