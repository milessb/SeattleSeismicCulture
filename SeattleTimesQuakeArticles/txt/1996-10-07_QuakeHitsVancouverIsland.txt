PALMER, Alaska - A powerful offshore earthquake shook parts of Canada's Vancouver Island yesterday, but there were no reports of damage or injuries, the Alaska Tsunami Warning Center said.    The quake had a preliminary magnitude of 6.0 and was centered in the Pacific Ocean about 70 miles southwest of Port Alice, B.C., which is at the north end of the island, and about 270 miles northwest of Seattle, said Paul Whitmore, a geophysicist at the center. The quake happened at 1:13 p.m.
