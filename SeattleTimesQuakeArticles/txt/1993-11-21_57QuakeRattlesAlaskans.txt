PALMER, Alaska - A moderate earthquake shook south-central Alaska yesterday, setting off alarms in the Anchorage area but resulting in no injuries or serious damage, authorities said.    Preliminary estimates indicated the quake registered 5.7 on the Richter scale of ground motion, said Alec Medbery of the Alaska Tsunami Warning Center. The epicenter was thought to be 130 miles southwest of Anchorage, Medbery said.
