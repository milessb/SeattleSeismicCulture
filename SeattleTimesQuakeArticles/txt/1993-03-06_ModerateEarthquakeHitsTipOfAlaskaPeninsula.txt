PALMER, Alaska      A moderate earthquake struck the tip of the Alaska Peninsula yesterday, but there were no immediate reports of any damage or injuries, officials with the Alaska Tsunami Warning Center said     The quake measured 5.2 on the Richter scale, a measure of ground motion as recorded on seismographs, and it occurred at 11:30 a.m., said Alex Medbery, a spokesman for the center. The quake's epicenter was 70 miles southwest of Sand Point, and it was felt by people in King Cove, Sand Point and Cold Bay, Medbery said.
