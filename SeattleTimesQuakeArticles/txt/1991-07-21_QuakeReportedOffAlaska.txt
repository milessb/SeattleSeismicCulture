WASHINGTON - A moderate earthquake occurred early yesterday off the Alaskan peninsula, the U.S. Geological Survey reported. There were no immediate reports of damage.     The quake, centered in the Gulf of Alaska about 40 miles southeast of Cold Bay, occurred at 4:49 a.m. PDT, said Geological Survey spokesman Don Finley.     Geological Survey earthquake monitors in Golden, Colo., said they recorded the tremor at a preliminary magnitude of 5.6 on the Richter scale.     The earthquake reportedly was felt in the communities of Cold Bay, King Cove and Sand Point, Alaska.
