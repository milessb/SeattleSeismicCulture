BUTTE, Mont. - A moderate earthquake struck a sparsely populated area of Montana and Idaho today, but there were no immediate reports of injuries or significant damage.    The quake, with a magnitude of 5.3, happened shortly before 8 a.m. near Lima in the extreme southwest corner of Montana, according to the earthquake-studies office at the Montana Bureau of Mines and Geology in Butte.    There was no report of damage at Yellowstone National Park. The epicenter is 70 miles west of the park's western edge.    Monique Lay of the state Disaster and Emergency Services Office said the quake was felt as far away as Billings, 200 miles to the east.
