EARTHQUAKE RATTLES NORTHERN CALIFORNIA    --  PITTSBURG, Calif.        A earthquake shook a wide area of Northern California yesterday evening, but there were no reports of damage or injury.        The earthquake was reported at 6:13 p.m. PDT and was felt over a huge area from Sacramento to the coastline and from Santa Cruz County to Solano County, an area perhaps 100 miles square.        The California Office of Emergency Services and the University of California at Berkeley seismological station both said the quake registered 4.2 on the Richter scale and was centered near the Contra Costa County community of Pittsburg, about 12 miles northeast of Concord.
