BELLINGHAM _ An earthquake measuring 3.5 on the Richter scale rattled an area south of Vancouver, B.C., yesterday afternoon.        The quake occurred at 1:10 p.m., said Chris Jonientz-Trisler of the University of Washington Geophysics Center. It was felt in Bellingham.         Royal Canadian Mounted Police said no reports of injuries or damage were received.        The Pacific Geoscience Center in Victoria, B.C., said the quake was centered in the south Surrey and White Rock area, south of Vancouver.        The Richter scale is a measure of ground motion recorded on seismographs. An earthquake of 3.5 on the Richter scale can cause slight damage.
