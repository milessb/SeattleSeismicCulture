KANTISHNA, Alaska - A small earthquake was recorded yesterday 75 miles northwest of the entrance of Denali National Park, the Alaska Earthquake Information Center said.     The quake, measuring 3.5 on the Richter scale, struck at 12:23 a.m. Alaska Standard Time. The epicenter was 20 miles south of the small town of Kantishna, said Guy Tytgat, a geophysicist at the earthquake center in Fairbanks.    No injuries or damage were reported.
