PALMER, Alaska     A moderate earthquake struck in the Aleutian islands last night, but there were no reports of injuries or damage, the Alaska Tsunami Warning Center said.    The quake happened at 9:58 p.m. Alaska daylight time, and a preliminary reading put its magnitude at 5.3 on the Richter scale, said Guy Urban of the warning center in Palmer.     It was centered about 340 miles east of Adak, in the Pacific Ocean, he said.    The quake did not generate a tsunami, or giant sea wave.
