PALMER, Alaska      A moderate earthquake early yesterday awakened many residents of Fairbanks but no damage was reported, the Alaska Tsunami Warning Center reported.    The temblor, which struck at 1:48 a.m., registered 4.8 on the Richter scale and was centered about 50 miles northwest of Fairbanks, center officials said.    The Richter scale is a measure of the magnitude of an earthquake.      An earthquake that registers 4 on the Richter scale can cause moderate damage. The quake that killed 131 people in Alaska in 1964 measured 8.4 on the scale.
