CHIGNIK, Alaska      A moderate earthquake hit Alaska yesterday afternoon, rattling some rural residents but causing no reported injuries or damage.     The 4.8 magnitude earthquake hit at 3:19 p.m., Alaska Daylight Time, and was centered 40 miles northeast of Chignik, said seismologist Wayne Jorgensen at the Tsunami Warning Center in Palmer, Alaska. It measured 4.8 on the Richter scale. An earthquake of 3.5 on the Richter scale can cause slight damage locally, a quake with a magnitude of 4 can cause moderate damage, and a quake measuring 5 or higher can cause considerable damage.     Chignik is a village on the Alaska Peninsula, about 450 miles southwest of Anchorage.
