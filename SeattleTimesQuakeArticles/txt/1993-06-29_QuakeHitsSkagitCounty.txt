SEDRO-WOOLLEY, Skagit County - A quake measuring 2.5 on the Richter scale shook people awake early today in Skagit County.     The shallow quake hit at 4:52 a.m., about six miles northwest of Sedro-Woolley, in an area where there have been a number of small quakes this year, said Bill Steele at the University of Washington Seismology Lab.     There were no injuries or reports of damage.
