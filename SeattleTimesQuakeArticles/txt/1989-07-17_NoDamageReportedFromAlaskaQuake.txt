NO DAMAGE REPORTED FROM ALASKA QUAKE    --  PALMER, Alaska        A moderate earthquake hit Alaska early yesterday, but no significant damage was reported, authorities said.        The quake measured 4.6 on the Richter scale, with its epicenter 20 miles north of Palmer, or 60 miles northeast of Anchorage, said Bill Zitek, a geophysicist at the Alaska Tsunami Warning Center.        The earthquake hit at 6:09 a.m., Zitek said, and was ``sharply felt'' in Palmer.        An earthquake of 3.5 on the Richter scale can cause slight damage in a local area; 4 can cause moderate damage.
