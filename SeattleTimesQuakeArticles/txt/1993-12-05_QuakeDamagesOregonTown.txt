KLAMATH FALLS, Ore. - A moderate earthquake heavily damaged at least one building in this southern Oregon community yesterday afternoon. There were no reports of injuries.     The quake struck about 2:20 p.m. and measured 5.4 on the Richter scale, according to the U.S. Geological Survey.  It was centered 10 miles northeast of Klamath Falls.     The quake partly collapsed the Arcade Hotel Building, which houses a restaurant, a comic-book shop and a bookstore.     Sam Hamilton, director of the Klamath County 911 communications center, said windows shattered and bricks fell out of several other buildings, including City Hall and the county library.     A Sept. 20 earthquake in the Klamath Falls area killed a motorist in a rock slide and damaged more than 100 buildings, homes and apartments. The county courthouse and library were declared unsafe.     That quake measured 5.7 to 5.9 on the Richter scale and was followed by several aftershocks, the largest measuring 6.0.      Shortly after 6 a.m. yesterday, a moderate earthquake was recorded near Prudhoe Bay in Alaska. It measured 5.7 on the Richter scale.      The quake was centered 60 miles southeast of Prudhoe Bay, at the foot of the Brooks mountain range,an area that gets few earthquakes.
