CHIGNIK, Alaska     A moderate earthquake that caused hanging plants to swing was felt yesterday by residents of this Alaska Peninsula community, but there were no reports of damage or injuries.     The Alaska Tsunami Warning Center in Palmer said the temblor measured 5.0 on the Richter scale and occurred at 12:56 p.m.     "We had several reports of plants swinging and people feeling a little rumbling," said Ron Bowers, chief of the Chignik Bay Fire Department.     The epicenter was in the Semidi Islands, about 450 miles southwest of Anchorage, said Guy Urban at the Tsunami Warning Center.
