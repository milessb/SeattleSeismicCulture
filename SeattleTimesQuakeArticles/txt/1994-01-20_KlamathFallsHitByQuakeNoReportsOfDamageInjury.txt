KLAMATH FALLS, Ore. - There were no immediate reports of damage or injuries yesterday after an earthquake rumbled through Oregon's Klamath Falls area.    The quake, measuring 4.4 on the Richter scale, struck at 2:28 p.m. and was centered 10 miles west-southwest of Klamath Falls, said Waverly Person, a geophysicist with the U.S. Geological Survey's National Earthquake Center in Golden, Colo.    Person said the quake is part of a series that has hit the area since a Sept. 20, 1993, temblor that measured 5.9 on the Richter scale and killed one person who was in a car hit by a large boulder.    Klamath County emergency dispatcher Rae Piazzini said there have been no reports of injuries or damage.
