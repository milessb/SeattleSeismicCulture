ANCHORAGE - An earthquake of magnitude 7.2 rattled the Aleutian Islands yesterday, one day after a stronger tremor sent hundreds of people rushing to high ground because of the threat of high waves.    No damage or injuries were reported from yesterday's quake. The epicenter  was in the Pacific about 30 miles southwest of Adak.     That was the third major quake in the Aleutians in the past three days; a temblor measuring 6.3 was recorded Saturday 65 miles southwest of Adak.    Sunday's 8:04 p.m. quake was centered about 60 miles west-southwest of Adak.
