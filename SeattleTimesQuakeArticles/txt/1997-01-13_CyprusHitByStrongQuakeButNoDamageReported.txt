NICOSIA, Cyprus - A strong earthquake shook the capital of Cyprus and other parts of the island today.     Buildings swayed violently during the 40-second tremor, but there were no reports of damage or injury.    The earthquake was felt as far away as Egypt, Lebanon, Jordan and Israel.    The epicenter was just south of the Cypriot town of Paphos, according to the Geophysical Institute of Israel, which said the quake had a magnitude of 6.2. Jordan's Earthquake Center said the magnitude was 5.8.    An earthquake with a magnitude of 5 or 6 can cause considerable damage.
