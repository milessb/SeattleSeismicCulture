SEATTLE - A small earthquake rattled an area near Mount Rainier yesterday.    The quake struck at 7:15 a.m. and had a magnitude of 3.2, the University of Washington seismology lab reported. It was centered 10 miles west of Mount Rainier, or about 14 miles east of Eatonville, and about 6 miles deep.    It was the second such temblor in four days in the Puget Sound area. On Wednesday, a magnitude 3.0 quake occurred southeast of Poulsbo, on the west side of Puget Sound.
