TOKYO (AP) _ A strong earthquake shook the northern Japanese islands of Honshu and Hokkaido today, the Central Meteorological Agency reported. Police said there were no immediate reports of damage or casualties        The quake had a preliminary reading of 6.8 on the Richter scale, Japanese authorities reported. It was the second strong earthquake in northern Japan in three days.        An announcement from the meteorological agency said the focus of the quake was located some 113 miles beneath the epicenter in northern Akita Prefecture, some 300 miles north of Tokyo.        The announcement said the quake was not expected to cause any tidal waves.        The meteorological agency said the quake registered 4 on the Japanese scale of 7 in Morioka and Hachinoe, both in the north of Japan's main island of Honshu, and 3 in Obihiro on Hokkaido, Japan's northernmost main island.        A tremor measuring 4 on the Japanese scale can shake houses. The scale is intended to show the intensity of ground motion at different locations.        The quake measured 2 in the Tokyo area, the agency said.        An earthquake also hit northern Japan yesterday. The Central Meteorological Agency said the quake, which registered 6.6 on the Richter scale, had its epicenter beneath Soviet-occupied Kunashiri Island, northeast of Hokkaido.        No damage or injury was reported in that quake.
