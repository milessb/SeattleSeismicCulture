BELLINGHAM - A small earthquake jolted some Whatcom County residents awake early yesterday.    The quake at 4:20 a.m. measured 2.8 on the Richter scale and was centered southeast of Deming, 13 miles east of Bellingham, the University of Washington geophysics center reported.    No damage was reported.    The Richter scale is a measure of ground motion as recorded on seismographs. An earthquake of 3.5 on the scale can cause slight damage.
