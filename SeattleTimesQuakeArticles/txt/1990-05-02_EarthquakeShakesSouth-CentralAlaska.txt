PALMER, Alaska        A strong earthquake shook south-central Alaska yesterday, but there were no immediate reports of damage or injuries.        The quake measured 6.4 on the Richter scale and struck at 8:15 a.m., according to the Alaska Tsunami Center. It was centered about 10 miles south of King Salmon, a village of 530 people about 280 miles southwest of Anchorage.        Alaska state police said there were no damage reports.
