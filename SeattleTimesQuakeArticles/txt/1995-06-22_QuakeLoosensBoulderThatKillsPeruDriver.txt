LIMA, Peru - A French journalist was killed yesterday when an earthquake dislodged a boulder that fell on his car.    There were no other immediate reports of injury or damage from the earthquake, which had a preliminary magnitude of 4.9 on the Richter scale and shook buildings in Lima and other coastal cities. It occurred at 11:36 a.m. and was centered below the Pacific Ocean, about 90 miles west of Lima.    Frederic Chappaz, 39, director of the Peru-Bolivia bureau of French news agency Agence France-Presse, was killed on a coastal highway, police said.    Chappaz was headed to the airport for a vacation. His three passengers weren't hurt, police said
